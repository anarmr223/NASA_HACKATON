SIMULATOR ASTROYER

1. The Visualizer: Trajectory and Consequences

Essentially, we have developed a new-generation 3D Interactive Visualizer. Using real orbital data from Near-Earth Objects (NEOs), we can accurately track the impact trajectory of any asteroid, such as the hypothetical "Impactor-2025".

The system goes beyond the impact point: it simulates the collision with our planet, identifying exactly where the strike would occur and determining which countries and populated areas would be affected by the resulting shockwave, earthquakes, and other catastrophic effects. It is a critical tool for understanding the magnitude of the threat.

2. The Problem We Solve: Warning and Mitigation

The main issue we address is the lack of rapid and accurate response. Our system is designed to:

Early Warning: Calibrate the remaining approach time when detecting the proximity of a hazardous object, activating early alert protocols.

Strategic Mitigation: Evaluate possible mitigation options. This includes determining the feasibility of a deflection attempt (altering its trajectory) or, ultimately, the need for an evacuation plan based on the predicted impact area.

3. Technological Arsenal: The Foundation of Precision

To achieve such a precise and visually immersive simulation, we rely on a powerful technological stack:

Databases and APIs: We use NASA's orbital APIs (specifically the NEO API) along with geological databases to feed the system with real-time asteroid trajectory and composition data.

3D Technology: The spatial models (such as the asteroid and Earth) were developed and textured using Blender. These models are integrated into the web frontend and rendered dynamically using cutting-edge libraries such as Three.js (for the main simulation) and Vanta.js (for the animated background and space atmosphere).
